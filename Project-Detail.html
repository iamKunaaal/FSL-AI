Session Description

This session introduces the paradigm shift from traditional intrusive advertising to "Respectful Engagement." Students will analyze how AI-driven algorithms personalize content and the ethical friction points regarding data privacy and manipulation.

Session 10 Block Period 1 · Challenge 7
Approved

Session Description

Students establish success criteria for a good marketing campaign (Ethical, Clear message, User-focused, Feasible, Creative), then generate ideas using rapid brainstorming, SCAMPER, and "worst possible idea" → flip techniques.

Ideas are grouped by theme and evaluated against the criteria matrix. Students link their best idea to SDG goals and social impact.

Session 13 Block Period 1 · Challenge 9
Approved

Session Description

Students test their campaigns with peers using a checklist (Is the message clear? Is it ethical? Would users trust it?). They observe reactions, collect feedback (likes/confusions/suggestions), and analyse what worked.

Introduces simple venture thinking — what value does the idea create, what resources are needed, and what ethical and practical risks exist.
